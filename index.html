Has Child Poverty in the US Improved in the Last Decade?

Latest figures show 12 million children still living in poverty.

By Salman Ahad

December 18, 2021

Credit: Joshua Hoehne.

Since 1990, the Annie E. Casey Foundation has been tracking data on the welfare of children in the US through their KIDS COUNT initiative. While COVID-19 undoubtedly had a major impact on children across the country, the latest figures we have at our disposal are from 2019, which shows Mississippi leading child poverty rates once again. Child poverty rates in the past decade peaked in 2012 when 16.3 million children were living in poverty, but declined to 12 million by 2019.

According to Dr. Linda Southward, co-director of Mississippi KIDS COUNT, "poverty is connected to children’s health and education, and disproportionately impacts children of color." She says that's one of the main reasons Mississippi continues to struggle. "Given the changing demographics in Mississippi, we have children who are more likely to be disenfranchised economically," she says, "which reinforces the importance of looking at children’s issues through an economic lens.”
